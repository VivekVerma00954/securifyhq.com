Remote CCTV Monitoring vs Security Guards: Cost & Coverage for NZ Businesses

Comparing remote CCTV monitoring and physical security guards for NZ businesses — cost, coverage, response time, and what actually makes sense for most sites.

← Back to Blog

When NZ businesses think about improving their security, two options come up most often: hire a security guard, or invest in a monitored camera system. Both have legitimate uses. But for most commercial sites — warehouses, construction sites, retail properties, and light industrial premises — the comparison looks very different once you work through the actual numbers and what each approach can and can't do.

This article breaks down the real cost and coverage trade-offs so you can make an informed decision for your site.

The True Cost of Physical Security Guards in NZ

Security guards in New Zealand typically cost between $25 and $45 per hour depending on the provider, the level of licence required, the hours of coverage, and whether overnight or weekend rates apply. For a single site requiring after-hours coverage from 6pm to 6am seven days a week, that's 84 hours per week of guarding.

At a mid-range rate of $35/hour, after-hours guarding alone runs to approximately $2,940 per week, or over $150,000 per year. That figure doesn't include callout fees for alarm responses, additional guards for large sites, or the administrative overhead of managing a security provider relationship.

Beyond cost, physical guards have structural limitations that are easy to overlook:

  • A single guard can only be in one location at a time — a large site with multiple entry points and camera feeds is impossible to fully cover on foot
  • Guards generate almost no documentation — most shift reports are brief logs, not structured incident records usable for insurance claims
  • Fatigue and attention drift are real factors during long overnight shifts
  • Sick leave, staff turnover, and short-notice substitutions are ongoing management challenges

What Remote CCTV Monitoring Costs

Remote monitoring connects your existing cameras to a system where AI pre-filters motion events and trained operators review genuine alerts in real time. At SecurifyHQ, after-hours monitoring starts from $199/month for sites with up to four cameras, with 24/7 options from $549/month.

At the entry level, that's roughly $2,400 per year for after-hours coverage — compared to $150,000+ for a full-time guard. Even the most comprehensive monitoring plan represents a fraction of the cost of physical guarding.

What Remote Monitoring Cannot Do

It would be misleading to present remote monitoring as a complete replacement for physical security in every scenario. There are things a camera system simply cannot do:

  • Physically intervene in an active incident
  • Provide a visible deterrent presence in the way a uniformed guard does
  • Conduct access control at entry points that require ID verification
  • Respond physically to a medical emergency on site

For high-risk sites with a history of violent incidents, or for events and venues requiring active crowd management, physical security personnel remain essential. The question is not which approach is better in absolute terms — it's which fills the gaps in your current setup at the right cost.

The Most Practical Approach for Most NZ Sites

For the majority of commercial properties in New Zealand — warehouses, construction sites, retail premises, light industrial, and professional offices — remote CCTV monitoring fills the most significant gap in existing security at a fraction of the cost of physical guarding.

📍 Common scenario

A Christchurch warehouse runs cameras but has no monitoring. After two break-ins in 18 months, the owner considers hiring a guard for overnight shifts. At $35/hour for a 12-hour shift, that's $420/night — $153,000/year. Remote after-hours monitoring at $349/month ($4,188/year) covers every camera, every night, with structured incident reports. The cost difference funds significant physical improvements to the site itself.

Where guard companies do add value alongside remote monitoring is in the escalation chain — when a monitoring operator verifies a genuine intrusion, dispatching a mobile patrol to physically attend the site is a natural next step. Legal and Liability Considerations

It's worth noting that in New Zealand, security guards must be licensed under the Private Security Personnel and Private Investigators Act 2010. Unlicensed security work carries legal risk for both the provider and the business. Remote monitoring services like SecurifyHQ operate under a different framework — as a monitoring and escalation service rather than a licensed security provider — and should be evaluated against your specific contractual and insurance requirements.

Structured incident documentation from a monitored system can also support insurance claims in ways that informal guard logs typically cannot, which has practical value beyond the immediate security benefit.

For most NZ commercial sites, remote monitoring closes the after-hours coverage gap at a cost that makes physical guarding look prohibitive. The two approaches complement each other when both are needed — monitoring identifies incidents, guards physically respond to them.
