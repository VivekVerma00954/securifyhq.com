Does Your Camera Work With SecurifyHQ?

The Basics

What is RTSP?

Understanding how your cameras stream video to our monitoring platform.

🎥 RTSP — Real Time Streaming Protocol

RTSP (Real Time Streaming Protocol) is the industry-standard protocol that allows IP cameras to stream live video to external systems — like monitoring platforms, NVRs, or software like Frigate AI.

Most professional IP cameras support RTSP out of the box. It's a standard feature on cameras from manufacturers like Hikvision, Dahua, Reolink, Axis, and Uniview. If your camera has an IP address and an ethernet or Wi-Fi connection, there's a good chance it supports RTSP.

Consumer cameras locked to a vendor app (like Ring, Eufy, or Arlo) typically do not expose an RTSP stream — the manufacturer keeps the stream locked inside their own cloud system. These are not compatible with SecurifyHQ.

Not Supported

Camera types we cannot connect

These cameras lock their video stream inside a proprietary cloud — there's no RTSP access available.

❌ Ring (Amazon)

Ring cameras are locked to Amazon's cloud ecosystem and do not expose an RTSP stream. No third-party monitoring platform can connect to them.

❌ Google Nest

Nest cameras require Google Home and do not support standard RTSP or ONVIF streaming. Incompatible with professional monitoring platforms.

❌ Amazon Blink

Blink devices are cloud-only and use proprietary protocols. No open RTSP access is available on any Blink camera model.

❌ Eufy / Arlo / Wyze

Consumer-grade cameras locked to their vendor apps. Stream access requires vendor cloud subscription — no open RTSP is exposed.

Not sure if your camera falls into this category? Contact us and we'll help you check before you commit to anything.

Check Your Setup

How to check if your camera is compatible

Three simple steps to confirm before you sign up.

1

Check your camera model

Search your camera's model number on the manufacturer's website. Look for "RTSP" in the specifications or technical sheet. Most professional IP cameras list it explicitly.

2

Look for an NVR or DVR

If your site has a Network Video Recorder (NVR) or Digital Video Recorder (DVR) with an ethernet port, it almost certainly supports RTSP — even if the cameras themselves are older analog units.

3

Not sure? Ask us.

We're happy to check compatibility before you commit to anything. Send us your camera model or a photo of your setup and we'll confirm within one business day.

FAQ

Camera compatibility questions

Do I need to buy new cameras to use SecurifyHQ?
Not necessarily. SecurifyHQ works with most professional IP cameras that support RTSP — the industry standard used by Hikvision, Dahua, Reolink, Uniview, Axis, and many others. If you already have compatible cameras installed, we can integrate them directly. We'll confirm compatibility during your initial consultation at no charge.
Why don't Ring or Google Nest cameras work with SecurifyHQ?
Ring, Google Nest, Amazon Blink, and similar cloud-only cameras are designed to work exclusively within their own closed ecosystems. They do not support RTSP or any open streaming protocol, which means third-party professional monitoring platforms cannot connect to them. You need cameras with RTSP support to use SecurifyHQ.
What internet speed do I need?
We recommend approximately 2 Mbps upload per camera for reliable continuous monitoring. For a 4-camera setup, that means at least 8–10 Mbps upload. Most NZ fibre and VDSL plans comfortably meet this. For construction sites on 4G, a good signal with 10+ Mbps upload typically supports up to 4 cameras. We'll review your connection during setup.
My cameras are older analog models — can you still connect them?
Yes, in most cases. If your analog cameras are connected to a Hikvision, Dahua, or similar IP-capable DVR/NVR with a network port, the recorder exposes one RTSP stream per camera channel. We connect to the DVR rather than the cameras directly — no camera replacement needed. Tell us your DVR model and we'll confirm.
How do you connect to my cameras securely?
As part of onboarding, we supply and configure a MikroTik VPN router pre-loaded with a WireGuard VPN tunnel. This creates an encrypted connection between your site and our monitoring platform — your camera streams are never exposed directly to the internet. Remote setup is handled by our team; no IT knowledge required on your end.
Can I use a 4G or Starlink connection?
Yes. SecurifyHQ works over any internet connection type including 4G/LTE, Starlink, VDSL, and fibre. 4G works well for 1–4 cameras with a good signal. Starlink is excellent for remote or rural construction sites — low latency and high bandwidth make it a strong choice. We'll confirm suitability during your setup consultation.
