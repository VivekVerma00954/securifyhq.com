Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about how SecurifyHQ works.

Getting Started
Most sites are operational within 3–5 business days of signup. We'll schedule a brief call to confirm your camera details, ship your VPN router, and walk you through the connection. Once your router is online and cameras are connected, monitoring begins immediately.
Pilot clients pay 50% off the setup fee (non-refundable) and get their first month free. Only 3 spots remaining — sign before 31 May 2026.
Almost certainly not. If your cameras support RTSP streaming (most IP cameras and analog cameras with an IP NVR do), we can monitor them. We install a small VPN router on-site that connects your cameras to our monitoring platform. You keep your existing cameras and recording setup.
Cameras & Compatibility
Any camera that supports RTSP streaming — this includes most commercial IP cameras, PoE cameras, and analog camera systems with an IP NVR (network video recorder). Cloud-locked cameras that only work through a vendor's app (like some basic solar 4G cameras) are not compatible, because they don't expose an RTSP feed. See our Camera Compatibility Guide for full details.
Yes, for sites with a 4G router and RTSP cameras. We install our VPN router on top of your existing 4G connection. 1–4 cameras stream reliably over 4G; for larger camera counts, a faster connection is recommended. Starlink also works well.
We install a MikroTik router on your site that creates a secure WireGuard VPN tunnel back to our monitoring server. This means your cameras never need to be exposed to the public internet — all traffic runs through the encrypted tunnel.
Yes. Our system reads the RTSP stream alongside your NVR/DVR. Your existing local recording setup is unchanged. We're adding monitoring, not replacing your storage.

Monitoring & Response
Our AI system detects the event and creates an alert. A human operator reviews the footage within our target time (3–10 minutes depending on your plan). If it's confirmed as a genuine incident, we immediately begin your escalation chain — calling your nominated contacts, and if pre-authorised, notifying a guard response company or police.
We don't offer contractual SLA guarantees, but we take our targets seriously and track performance internally. Our targets are: Watch (10 min), Respond (7 min), Deter (5 min), Shield (3 min). These are measured from the moment the AI alert arrives to when an operator classifies it.
Camera disconnections are automatically detected and create a separate support ticket. You'll receive a notification with recommended troubleshooting steps. Our team will assist remotely to get the camera back online.
Shield plan clients can pre-authorise SecurifyHQ to notify relevant emergency services or authorities on their behalf when a verified security incident occurs — without needing to be reachable at that moment. This pre-authorisation is captured digitally as part of your service agreement and can be updated at any time.
Working Alongside Your Existing Setup
No — and we'd recommend you don't. Your alarm company and SecurifyHQ serve different purposes. Your alarm triggers a siren and (if monitored) alerts an alarm company that a sensor was tripped. SecurifyHQ tells you what is actually happening — a real break-in or a false alarm, what the intruder looks like, and where they are on your site. Most of our clients keep both: their alarm handles compliance and the first deterrent, and SecurifyHQ provides the visual verification and faster, better-informed response that an alarm company can't.
SecurifyHQ is a verified monitoring and notification service — not a registered alarm monitoring company. If your insurer requires alarm monitoring (many commercial policies do), that requirement is met by your existing alarm company — keep them running. SecurifyHQ sits on top of that as a visual layer: documented incident footage and verified alert reports that strengthen your insurance position after an event. We'd encourage you to notify your broker that you've added professional CCTV monitoring with incident documentation — some insurers view this positively when assessing risk.
We escalate. If our operator activates the 2-way audio speaker and the person doesn't leave, the incident moves immediately to your escalation chain: your nominated keyholder is called first, then your site manager or other emergency contact. On Shield and Pro Plus plans, if you've pre-authorised it, we can also notify a guard response company or emergency services directly on your behalf. The audio deterrent is the first line — it's not the last.
Privacy & Data
Event clips (triggered by AI detection) are stored on our secure cloud infrastructure hosted on DigitalOcean. Servers are located outside New Zealand — see our Privacy Policy for details. Continuous recording is optional. You control how long clips are retained (default: 30 days). Footage is never shared with third parties.
Yes. We operate under NZ Privacy Act 2020, including Information Privacy Principle 12 for cross-border data handling. Our operators are bound by our internal data handling policies and confidentiality agreements.
