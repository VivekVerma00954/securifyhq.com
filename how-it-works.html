How It Works

From camera trigger to incident report — here's exactly what happens

No black boxes. No vague promises. Here is the full picture of what happens when your cameras pick up something suspicious — from first detection to your doorstep notification.

The Process

Five steps. Every verified alert.

Every alert follows the same structured process — no skipped steps, no exceptions, full audit trail throughout.

1
Detection

Your cameras detect motion or an event

SecurifyHQ connects to your existing RTSP-capable cameras over a secure network connection. When Frigate (our AI motion analysis layer) detects a person, vehicle, or defined object in a monitored zone, an alert is created in our system instantly.

  • Works with any RTSP-capable camera — Hikvision, Dahua, Reolink, Axis, and others
  • No new hardware required in most cases — just a secure network path to your cameras
  • AI pre-filter reduces false alerts before any human sees them
  • Alert logged with timestamp, camera ID, property, and clip reference
2
Human Review

A trained operator reviews the footage

The alert appears in our operator console. A human operator — not an algorithm — reviews the clip within minutes and makes a determination: is this a real security event, or a false alarm?

  • Operator reviews live or recorded clip directly in the alert console
  • False alarms (animals, shadows, deliveries) are logged and dismissed with reason
  • True alerts are escalated immediately — operator adds notes and starts the escalation clock
  • Every decision is timestamped and visible in your incident report
3
Escalation

Your escalation plan runs automatically

If the alert is verified as real, your pre-configured escalation plan kicks in — set once during onboarding, runs automatically every time without the operator needing to decide in the moment.

1

Contact 1 — Call & message

Your first contact (usually you or your site manager) is called and messaged simultaneously. Timeout = your chosen wait time.

2

Contact 2 — No response fallback

If Contact 1 doesn't respond within your timeout, Contact 2 is notified automatically. All steps logged.

3

Guard company (if authorised)

If you've connected a physical guard service and pre-authorised this step, they are contacted for dispatch.

!

Emergency Services (pre-authorised or life-threatening)

If you've pre-authorised emergency service contact, or if there is evidence of a life-threatening situation, we notify the relevant authorities as a reporting party on your behalf.

⚠ Life-threatening override: if there is credible evidence of physical danger to a person on site, we contact 111 regardless of your escalation settings. This cannot be disabled.

4
Documentation

A full incident report is generated

Once an alert is resolved, a PDF incident report is auto-generated. This is a structured legal document — not a summary email — useful for insurance claims, police follow-up, and your own records.

5
Reporting

Monthly summary — automatically

At the end of each month (Respond plan and above), you receive an automated monitoring summary: all alerts, false alarm rate, camera uptime, response times. A running record of your site security performance.

  • Delivered by email and available in your client portal
  • Tracks response time trends, camera uptime, alert frequency
  • Useful for risk assessments, insurance renewals, and site reviews
